What is a Convolutional Neural Network?

Before we tell you what it is, think about how you are seeing this webpage right now. How does your brain know what a computer or a website look like? Well its because you have learned (since your youth) what a computer is. You also know that a your smartphone and a piece of rectangular cardboard (of roughly the same shape) are two distinct objects. But naively, you can think of them as having dimensions and are therefore the same. To an extreme, you also managed to learn nuanced differences; say between an Android phone and an iPhone even though they are quite similar sometimes.

Convolutional neural networks are a form of deep learning meaning that the computers model very complex features and are fed LOTS of data. Over time, the computer learns how to discriminate between images, sounds, DNA or just about any data you can think about. With the cheap cost of computational power of graphics processing units nowadays (thanks, gamers!), we can afford to build very complex models which are increasingly better than traditional machine learning models. These models have layers upon layers upon layers of abstraction making it very good at learning very complex things. Today, convolutional neural networks are running many popular social media sites which use image classification (like Google, Facebook, Snapchat, and so on).

Convolutional neural networks uses a bunch of filters (think Instagram!) which allows it to easily detect corners and edges in an image. Not only that; deep in the network the neural network can detect entire faces by combining filters heirarchically. It's like pieceing together a very large puzzle from very small pieces and then guessing what that puzzle is. What kinds of filters do we need to select for the neural network? Nothing! In fact, the convolutional neural network will learn these filters as they go along, creating the best filter to be able to classify whatever image it was trained to see.

How was this made?

We scraped some images of fashionable items from the UAE Souq.com website and trained a convolutional neural network. The libraries we used include Keras, TensorFlow and a lot of GPU power. This web application is made using Flask and Python.
